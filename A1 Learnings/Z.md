# Z

In Spanish, the letter Z follows one simple rule: it has one sound, regardless of which vowel follows it.   
However, that sound changes based on geography:

## 1. The Regional Rule

**Latin America & Philippines (Seseo)**: The Z is always pronounced like a simple English "S".

    Zapato (Shoe) — sah-pah-toh
**Most of Spain (Distinción)**: The Z is always pronounced like the "TH" in "think."

    Zapato (Shoe) — thah-pah-toh

## 2. The Spelling Rule (The "Z" to "C" Swap)
In Spanish, **Z** almost never appears before the vowels **E** or **I**.    
If a word needs that "S/TH" sound before an **E** or **I**, the **Z** changes to a **C**.

### Example: Singular to Plural
Lápiz (Pencil) — ends in **Z**  
Lápices (Pencils) — the **Z** changes to **C** because it is now followed by **E**

### Example: Verbs
Empezar (To start) — has a **Z**    
Empecé (I started) — the **Z** changes to **C** because of the **E**

## 3. "Z" at the End of a Word
When a word ends in **Z**, the sound is usually very faint or "clipped."

    Pez (Fish) — pehs
    Luz (Light) — loos
    Feliz (Happy) — feh-lees
